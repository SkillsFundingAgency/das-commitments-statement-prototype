Things you need to know

Before starting your apprenticeship your training provider should have:

  • told you if you'll be working with any other training providers during your apprenticeship
  • given you a day-to-day contact who can answer any questions you might have

Your training provider is responsible for your off-the-job training, which you’ll be assessed against at the end of your apprenticeship by an independent assessor.

You’ll spend at least 20% of your working hours doing off-the-job training. This may be with your training provider or at your place of work.
