Apprentice

These roles and responsibilities set out what is expected of you.

I have:

  • read and signed my apprenticeship agreement
  • been told when my apprenticeship starts and finishes
  • understood the roles of my employer and training provider
  • understood that I will be assessed by an independent end-point-assessment organisation
  • read through my training plan with my employer and training provider
  • understood that at least 20% of my apprenticeship hours will be off-the-job training

I will:

  • take part in regular progress reviews with my employer and provider
  • go to all my off-the-job training and give evidence where needed
  • complete all my learning activities, assignments and exams
  • tell my employer in advance when I cannot attend work
  • tell my training provider in advance when I cannot attend off the job training
  • agree with my employer and training provider when I am ready to complete my end point assessment
  • tell my employer and training provider about any learning support or health issues
  • raise any concerns or complaints through my employer and provider’s official channels and take my complaint further if I need to

Your employer

The roles and responsibilities set out what is expected of your employer.

They have:

  • signed an apprenticeship agreement between you–the apprentice–and them
  • contributed to a training plan with you and your training provider
  • committed to working with your training provider to support your training needs

They will:

  • enable you to complete your off-the-job training (20% minimum requirement) during your working hours
  • take part in regular progress reviews with you and your training provider
  • work with you and your training provider to resolve any concerns or complaints

Your training provider

The roles and responsibilities set out what is expected of your training provider.

They have:

  • a copy of the apprenticeship agreement signed by you–the apprentice–and your employer
  • told you and your employer when the apprenticeship starts and ends
  • explained how off-the-job training will be delivered
  • told you and your employer about whether they will use sub-contracted training providers
  • explained that you be assessed by an end-point assessment organisation
  • signed a training plan with you and your employer
  • discussed the training plan you and ensured it accounts for prior learning, English and maths needs and additional learning support

They will:

  • manage the quality and delivery of your off-the-job training
  • take responsibility for regular progress reviews between all parties
  • make sure that you and your employer know how to address concerns or complaints about the apprenticeship
