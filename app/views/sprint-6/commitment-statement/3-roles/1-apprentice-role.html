Apprentice

The roles and responsibilities set out what is expected of you.

I have:

  • read through the details of my signed apprenticeship agreement
  • understood the key dates and milestones of my apprenticeship, including my planned start, my planned end, and my end-point assessment
  • understood the roles of my employer, training provider and end-point-assessment organisation
  • understood the information about how my apprenticeship will be assessed by the end-point-assessment organisation
  • been taken through a training plan for my apprenticeship and received a copy of the training plan that I have signed together with my employer and training provider
  • understood that at least 20% of my apprenticeship hours will be used for off-the-job training

You will:

  • take part in regular progress reviews with my employer and provider
  • attend all learning and development activities, giving evidence where needed
  • let my employer or work & training provider know in advance when I cannot attend
  • agree with my employer and training provider when I am ready to complete my assessment
  • raise any concerns or complaints through my employer and provider’s official channels and take my concern or complaint further if I need to
  • go to all your off-the-job training and workshops (you must let your training provider or employer know straight away if you cannot go)
  • complete the learning activities for each module, including any self-study or research
  • complete all your coursework, assignments and exams
  • give your training provider any evidence of off-the-job training if they ask for it
  • go to all your progress review meetings with your employer or training provider
  • let your employer or training provider know if you have any learning support or health issues

Employer

The roles and responsibilities set out what is expected of your employer.

They have:

  • a signed apprenticeship agreement between you–the apprentice–and them
  • contributed to a training plan with you and your training provider
  • understood and fully support your training needs, and will work with your training provider to ensure these are met

They will:

  • enable you to complete your off-the-job training (20% minimum requirement) during the working hours defined in the apprenticeship agreement
  • take part in regular progress reviews with you and your training provider
  • support you and your training provider in addressing and resolving any concerns or complaints

Provider

The roles and responsibilities set out what is expected of your training provider.

They have:

  • a copy of the apprenticeship agreement signed by you–the apprentice–and your employer
  • given the key dates and milestones of the apprenticeship to you and your employer, including your planned start, planned end, expected apprenticeship duration and end-point assessment date
  • explained how off-the-job training will be delivered, including the use of sub-contracted training providers where applicable
  • explained how the apprenticeship will be assessed by the end-point assessment organisation
  • provided and signed a training plan with you and your employer
  • discussed the training plan with all parties, ensuring it takes account of your prior learning, English and maths needs and additional learning support

They will:

  • manage the delivery and quality of your off-the-job training, as agreed in the training plan
  • be responsible for regular progress reviews between you, your employer and themselves
  • make you and your employer aware of how they should address concerns or complaints regarding the apprenticeship
