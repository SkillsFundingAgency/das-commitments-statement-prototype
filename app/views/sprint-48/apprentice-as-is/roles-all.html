Roles and responsibilities

Read through the roles and responsibilities for you, your employer and your training provider.

Your responsibilities as an apprentice

These roles and responsibilities set out what is expected of you.

I have:

  • read and signed my apprenticeship agreement
  • discussed my training plan with my current employer and my training provider
  • told my current employer and my training provider about any learning needs or health issues I have
  • been told when my apprenticeship training starts and ends
  • understood that this is a portable flexi-job apprenticeship and that I will move between different jobs during my apprenticeship
  • understood the roles of my employers and training provider during my apprenticeship
  • understood that I need to complete the off-the-job training hours stated in my training plan during my normal working hours
  • understood that I will be assessed by an end-point assessor at the end of my apprenticeship training

I will:

  • take part in regular progress reviews with my employer and training provider every 12 weeks
  • attend all my off-the-job training and provide evidence of my recorded hours where needed
  • complete all of my learning activities, assignments and exams
  • share my learning and progress to date with each new employer
  • find my next job when the current job ends, until I am ready to complete my end-point assessment
  • tell my employer in advance when I cannot attend work
  • tell my training provider in advance when I cannot attend my off-the-job training
  • agree with my employer and training provider when I am ready to complete my end-point assessment
  • raise any concerns or complaints through my employer and training providers

Your employer's responsibilities

The roles and responsibilities set out what is expected of your employer.

They have:

  • signed and provided you with a copy of your apprenticeship agreement that you agreed together
  • contributed to a training plan with you and your training provider
  • committed to working with you and your training provider to support your learning needs
  • agreed your employment start and end dates with a minimum duration of 3 months

They will:

  • enable you to complete your off-the-job training during your normal working hours as defined in your training plan
  • take part in regular progress reviews with you and your training provider every 12 weeks
  • work with you and your training provider to resolve any concerns or complaints
  • carry out an end of employment review when your job ends

Your training provider's responsibilities

The roles and responsibilities set out what is expected of your training provider.

They have:

  • a copy of the apprenticeship agreement signed by you and your employer
  • discussed with you how your portable flexi-job apprenticeship will be delivered
  • discussed your training plan with you and your employer and ensured it accounts for prior learning, English and maths needs and additional learning support
  • told you and your employer your apprenticeship training start and end dates
  • explained how off-the-job training will be delivered
  • told you and your employer if they will use sub-contracted training providers to deliver any of your training
  • explained that you will be assessed by an end-point assessment organisation

They will:

  • manage the quality and delivery of your off-the-job training and making sure it is logged
  • support you in finding jobs as you progress through your apprenticeship
  • take responsibility for regular progress reviews between all parties every 12 weeks
  • make sure that you and each employer can see a summary of your learning and progress to date
  • make sure that you and your employer know how to raise concerns or complaints about the apprenticeship training
  • maintain contact with you if you are on an extended break in learning
