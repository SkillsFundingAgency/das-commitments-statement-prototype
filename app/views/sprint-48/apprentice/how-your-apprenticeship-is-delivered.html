How your apprenticeship will be delivered

Your portable flexi-job apprenticeship will be delivered in two parts:

  • a training period when you will move between different jobs
  • an end-point assessment

Your training period

Your employers will give you hands-on experience in your chosen occupation. They'll provide you with training and supervision to develop the skills for the role.

Each individual employer will help you develop some of the skills required for your apprenticeship. When a job ends, you'll need to find a new job to develop more of the required skills.

Your training provider is responsible for your off-the-job training. Your required hours for off-the-job training are stated in your training plan. This is an agreement between you, your provider and your employer. You'll spend off-the-job training time developing knowledge, skills and behaviours for your apprenticeship.

Your training plan will set out a schedule for your off-the-job training. Your training plan will include:

  • the content and modules you will cover in off-the-job training
  • how and when your off-the-job training will be delivered
  • English and maths qualifications, if you do not already have these
  • regular progress reviews with your employer and training provider

Your end-point assessment

You'll be assessed by an independent assessor at the end of your apprenticeship. They will assess the knowledge, skills and behaviours you need to be competent in your role.

You will have your end-point assessment when you, your employer and training provider agree that you're ready. You will discuss this in your progress reviews.

Your employer and training provider will tell you what to expect and when the assessment will take place.
