How your apprenticeship will be delivered

Your portable flexi-job apprenticeship will be delivered in two parts:

  • a training period when you will move between different jobs
  • an end-point assessment

Your training period

Your employers will give you hands-on experience in your chosen occupation. They'll provide you with training and supervision to help you develop the skills you need to perform the role you've been employed to do.

Each individual employer will help you develop some of the skills required for your apprenticeship. When a job ends, you'll need to find a new job to develop more of the required skills.

Your training provider is responsible for your off-the-job training. Your required hours for off-the-job training are stated in your training plan as agreed by you, your provider and each employer. You'll spend this time learning new knowledge, skills and behaviours for your apprenticeship. Your training provider will manage your off-the-job training.

Your training plan is agreed between you and your training provider, with input from your employers at the start of each job. The plan will set out a training schedule for your off-the-job training. Your training plan will include:

  • the delivery of the knowledge, skills and behaviours required for your apprenticeship
  • how and when your off-the-job training will be delivered
  • English and maths qualifications, if you have not already achieved these
  • regular progress reviews with your employer and training provider

Your end-point assessment

You'll be assessed by an independent end-point assessor on the knowledge, skills and behaviours that you've learnt during your apprenticeship to confirm you're occupationally competent.

You will agree when you're ready for your end-point assessment in your progress reviews with your employer and training provider.

Your employer and training provider will tell you what to expect and when the assessment will take place.
